The Ship of Theseus thought problem challenges our intuitions about identity and change. It raises questions about what makes something the same over time, and whether it's possible for an object to remain the same despite changes to its constituent parts. It's a paradox that has puzzled philosophers for centuries, and there's no straightforward answer.
